Are You Ready to Play?

Here's some basics...

What your turn will look like.

Begin
Untap lands and creatures so they may become usuable again
Draw a card (must have 7 cards or less in hand)
Main Phase
Play a land (only one per turn)
Cast creatures and/or sorceries
Combat
Declare attackers
Opponent declares blockers
Combat damage is dealt
Second Main Phase
Play a land (if you haven't already)
Cast creatures and/or sorceries
End
Your creatures heal
Discard any extra cards in hand
Pass the turn to your opponent

The different styles of decks, ranked as best to worst by ME!

  1. Midrange: a combonation of both aggro and control, midrange decks have cards that continuously generate value throughout the game
  2. Combo: these decks rely on synergy between cards, or how well one can activate the other - they usually try to win in a couple turns, focusing on a single type of combo
  3. Aggro: lots of low-cost cards that deal damage quickly, before the opponent can react
  4. Control: control decks are all about disrupting the opposing attempt to play the game, usually with compounding sorceries and use of "the stack" (the order in which spells resolve)

Here's the different types of cards (called "spells")...

  • Permanents: Lands, creatures, and basically anything that doesn't resolve and go into your graveyard immediately after casting.
  • Instants: You can play these at any time. It's usually useful to have enough mana (untapped lands) left over so they are "quickdrawable."
  • Sorceries: These spells are generally more powerful than instants, and can only be played during your turn.
